🔎 What you're seeing
Each bar in the sampling distribution is one survey result. After many surveys, the bars form a bell curve — even though the population distributions above are skewed, bimodal, or binary. That's the CLT.
↕ Try changing sample size
Slide n down to 10 and run 500 surveys — the sampling distribution is wide. Now slide to 500: the bell curve tightens dramatically around the true mean. This is the Law of Large Numbers working alongside the CLT.
± Margin of Error
The margin of error (±1.96 SE) tells you where 95% of all possible survey results will land. It shrinks as √n grows — doubling the precision requires quadrupling your sample size. Now you know why polling is expensive.
